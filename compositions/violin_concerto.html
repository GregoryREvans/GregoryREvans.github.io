Violin Concerto

score





The following inscription is found at the head of the score:
This concerto makes use of a Jazz Ensemble rather than an Orchestra in the spirit of Gunther Schuller’s championing of Jazz as an important and sustainable genre of serious music. Time is skewed in this piece to deliver a multiplicity of tempo and time perspectives in a kind of Independence Counterpoint. The Soloist seeks to assert itself over its comrades but fails to do so, instead, melting into the unified society of ensemble. The personality of each individual contributes to the larger scale of the ensemble’s entity.

This concerto is written for violinist Gabriela Spampinato.


Instrumentation:

Solo Violin
Clarinet in Bb
Tenor Saxophone
Baritone Saxophone
Trumpet in Bb
